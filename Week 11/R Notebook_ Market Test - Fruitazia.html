Market test

In this notebook we will explore how statistical analysis can help us determine advertising effectiveness using market test data.

Data

We will work with fruitazia.csv file. The data presents results of a market test for fruit juice with 2 advertising levels ×× 3 price levels, which gives us 6 experimental conditions, with 5 supermarkets per condition.

# loading data

data$AdSpend <- as.factor(data$AdSpend)

ANOVA - no interaction terms

Here is how we perform basic analysis of variance using lm() function. All effects are highly statistically significant at 0.050.05 level. When we use anova() command on the lm() output, we obtain information about the overall significance of each factor, rather than of a specific level of a factor.

model <- lm(Sales ~ Price + AdSpend, data=data)
summary(model)

ANOVA - with interactions

Here is how we perform the same analysis with interaction terms between ad spend and price levels added in. We see that advertising is less effective at higher price levels. This interaction effect is statistically significant at 0.050.05 level only for $5.9$5.9 price.

model1 <- lm(Sales ~ Price * AdSpend, data=data)
